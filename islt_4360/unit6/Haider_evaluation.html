I was also told on two separate occasions that I left out the footer on two of my pages, I'm not sure how I missed this but I will be sure to fix this immediately. I was also informed that the border on my contact form intersects with the image on my contact page and I will fix this right away. One of my classmates recommended that I put the image in the header which I can't wait to try, I think this will help a lot.

Recommended/Planned Changes to the Design

I plan to change several elements of my website including:

  • Footer

    • Alice Yanello and one of my classmates (in our class discussion) both noted that I had left out a footer on two of my pages. I will include this with my reasoning being that people will need to see my information on each page to reach me easier and to provide credibility and professionalism to my site.

  • Image description/add pages

    • One of my classmates pointed out that the images were intriguing but she wanted to know more about them. I also received feedback from Alice and Andrew that they would like to learn more about my content and see more. This change will help me to reach more of my users in a more engaging way and will improve my site by making it easier to use and read.

  • Put image in header/fix contact form border

    • One of my classmates noted that my "icy stare" image intersects with the boarder on my contact form. I will attempt to resize this image so that it is not in the way to provide a more visually appealing experience for my users.

  • Change navigation

    • The biggest feedback I recevied through my evaluations and class discussion was about my navigation element. Both Andrew and the classmate that reviewed my site said that the text and white background makes the menu tougher to look at and use. I'm so happy that I've asked them to review because I previously didn't realize this was an issue, I plan to fix this and instead I will use heavier weighted text with a different color that allows for a lot of contrast. I hope to make my navigation more user friendly which will improve the site by allowing more users to (hopefully) click on the contact form!

Reflection on Your Evaluation Experience

I found the most useful evaluation to be from classmates in our discussion board, through my personal observations and through my interview form. I found that the Evaluation form was tough for people to fill out and have the time to do (I sent to 10 people and only was able to get 3 to fill it out), whereas when I created a survey on Microsoft Forms people were a lot more willing to help because it wasn't as time consuming for them, and their responses were more substantive. In the evaluation form, it seemed like people were trying to rush through it. Not sure why a Word doc is more "intimidating" than a Microsoft Forms survey, but I'll be sure to keep this in mind when I'm doing research in the future.

I learned a lot of things that I didn't pick up myself, that giving people context (on my pictures for example) is the most important element of my site because my users would like to be walked through the content, a website is different than an art gallery where you need to explain the intended purpose of your website. I also learned that my attention to detail isn't as fine tuned as I thought, as I left out my footer on two pages, had an image intersecting with the border on my contact form and even had a grammatical error on my home page. I will work hard to fix these prior to submitting my final project. I learned that my classmates are by far the best resource, they know what the expectations of the project are and are more familiar with HTML than anyone else I have asked (perhaps I need to do some networking).

My different evaluators all brought various expertise to the table because they had different backgrounds. Andrew has a background with innovation, technology and HTML while Alice has a background in recruiting, user experience and psychology and Lauren has a journalistic background with graphic design.
